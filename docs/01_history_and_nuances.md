# 1. Unlocking the French Accent & Politeness

Before learning what to say in Paris, it is crucial to learn *how* to say it. The French language is highly structured around politeness. A simple phrase delivered with a smile and the correct level of respect opens doors.

## 1. The Golden Rule of Paris
If you remember only one thing from this guide: **Never speak to a stranger in France without first saying *Bonjour***. 

In English-speaking countries, it's normal to approach a shop worker and simply ask, "Where is the bathroom?" In France, this is considered extremely rude. You must establish a polite greeting first.

* **Bonjour** ⟨bohn-ZHOOR⟩ — Hello / Good morning.
* **Bonsoir** ⟨bohn-SWAHR⟩ — Good evening (used after 5 PM or when it gets dark).

## 2. The Two Forms of "You" (Tu vs. Vous)
French has two ways to say "you." Getting this right is the second most important rule.

| Concept | English Concept | French Word | When to use it |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Formal You** | Speaking to a boss, elder, or stranger (Sir/Madam) | **Vous** ⟨VOO⟩ | ALWAYS use this with waiters, shopkeepers, and strangers. |
| **Informal You** | Speaking to a close friend, child, or pet | **Tu** ⟨TOO⟩ | Only use if invited to, or with people your own age at a casual party. |

## 3. Pronunciation Hacks for English Speakers

French has sounds that don't exist in English. But you can get close enough to be understood.

### The Infamous French "R"
The French "R" (IPA: /ʁ/) happens at the back of the throat, almost like a soft gargle or a very gentle "H". It is *not* rolled like in Spanish, and your tongue should *not* curl back like in English.
* Try saying the English "K" sound, but let air pass through continuously instead of stopping it. That’s the spot.
* *Example:* **Merci** ⟨mair-SEE⟩ (Make the R very soft).

### The French "U"
English speakers usually pronounce the French "U" like the "oo" in *food*. That is incorrect.
* **The Trick:** Say "eeee" (like in *cheese*) and hold it. While holding the "eeee" sound, round your lips tightly as if you're whistling. 
* *Example:* **Tu** ⟨TOO⟩ (But with rounded, tight lips).

### Silent Final Letters
As a general rule, the final consonants of French words (S, T, D, X, P, Z) are **not pronounced**.
* *Paris* is pronounced ⟨pah-REE⟩.
* *Comment* is pronounced ⟨koh-MAHN⟩.
