# 9. Vocabulary Multipliers

Here is a massive shortcut: Thousands of English and French words are identical (or nearly identical) because English borrowed heavily from Norman French. By learning a few suffix conversions, you instantly "know" thousands of French words.

## 1. The English "-tion" = The French "-tion"
Words ending in "-tion" in English usually end in exactly the same way in French. The spelling is identical, just pronounce it with a French accent ⟨syon⟩.

* Information -> **Information** ⟨an-fohr-mah-syon⟩
* Nation -> **Nation** ⟨nah-syon⟩
* Attention -> **Attention** ⟨ah-tahn-syon⟩
* Position -> **Position** ⟨poh-zee-syon⟩

## 2. The "-ty" to "-té" Rule
Many English words ending in "-ty" end in "-té" in French.
* University -> **Université** ⟨oo-nee-vair-see-tay⟩
* Activity -> **Activité** ⟨ahk-tee-vee-tay⟩
* Reality -> **Réalité** ⟨ray-ah-lee-tay⟩
* Society -> **Société** ⟨soh-syay-tay⟩

## 3. The "-ly" to "-ment" Rule (Adverbs)
When you want to turn an adjective into an adverb (like adding "-ly" in English), you often add "-ment" in French.
* Slow -> Slowly | Lent -> **Lentement** ⟨lahnt-mahn⟩
* Rapid -> Rapidly | Rapide -> **Rapidement** ⟨rah-peed-mahn⟩
* Exact -> Exactly | Exact -> **Exactement** ⟨eg-zahkt-mahn⟩

## 4. Useful Core Prefixes
* **re- / r-** : To do again. 
  * *Faire* (to do) -> **Refaire** ⟨ruh-fer⟩ (to redo).
* **dé- / dés-** : The opposite / to undo (like "un-" in English).
  * *Faire* (to do) -> **Défaire** ⟨day-fer⟩ (to undo).
* **in- / im-** : Not / Opposite.
  * *Possible* -> **Impossible** ⟨an-poh-seebl⟩. 

## 5. False Friends (Faux Amis)
Beware! Some words look identical but mean totally different things.
* **Actuellement**: Means "Currently" or "Right now", NOT "Actually".
* **Attendre**: Means "To wait", NOT "To attend".
* **Préservatif**: Means "Condom", NOT food preservative!
* **Coin**: Means "Corner", NOT money.
