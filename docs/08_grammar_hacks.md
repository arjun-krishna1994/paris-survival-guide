# 8. Grammar Hacks & Sentence Building

You don't need to master French grammar to survive Paris. You just need a few functional building blocks.

## 1. Asking Questions
In textbooks, you'll learn complex question inversions. On the streets of Paris, they just use **Est-ce que** or tone of voice.

* **Method 1: The "Est-ce que" blanket.** Just put ⟨es-kuh⟩ in front of any statement to turn it into a question.
  * Statement: *Tu parles anglais.* (You speak English).
  * Question: **Est-ce que** *tu parles anglais?* (Do you speak English?)

* **Method 2: Voice inflection.** Just raise your pitch at the end of the sentence. Very common in casual speech.
  * *Vous avez l'addition?* ⟨voo-zah-vay lah-dee-syon⟩ — You have the check?

## 2. The Three Magic Verbs
If you know *To be* (Être), *To have* (Avoir), and *To go* (Aller), you can express 80% of what you need.

| Concept | Être (To Be) | Avoir (To Have) | Aller (To Go) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** (Je) | **suis** ⟨swee⟩ | **ai** ⟨ay⟩ | **vais** ⟨vay⟩ |
| **You** formal (Vous) | **êtes** ⟨et⟩ | **avez** ⟨ah-vay⟩ | **allez** ⟨ah-lay⟩ |
| **You** casual (Tu) | **es** ⟨ay⟩ | **as** ⟨ah⟩ | **vas** ⟨vah⟩ |
| **It is** (C'est) | **C'est** ⟨say⟩ | - | - |

## 3. Building Sentences: "I would like..."
The phrase **Je voudrais** ⟨zhuh voo-dray⟩ is your best friend. It means "I would like" and it is infinitely more polite than "Je veux" (I want). 

* **Je voudrais un croissant.** — I would like a croissant.
* **Je voudrais payer.** — I would like to pay.
* **Je voudrais aller au Louvre.** — I would like to go to the Louvre.

## 4. The Magic "Y" (There)
French uses the pronoun *y* to replace places, meaning "there."
* *Je vais à Paris.* -> **J'y vais.** ⟨zhee vay⟩ — I'm going there.
* **On y va!** ⟨ohn ee vah⟩ — Let's go! (Super common phrase).
