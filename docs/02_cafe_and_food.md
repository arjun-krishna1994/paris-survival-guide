# 2. Café Culture & Ordering Food

Paris is famous for its café culture. Whether you want a strong espresso or a glass of wine, knowing how to order smoothly will make your experience vastly better. 

## 1. Entering and Seating
When you enter a café or restaurant, wait to be seated or ask the waiter if a table is free.

* **Bonjour, une table pour deux, s'il vous plaît.** ⟨bohn-zhoor, oon tah-bl poor duh, seel voo play⟩ — Hello, a table for two, please.
* **Est-ce qu'on peut s'asseoir ici ?** ⟨es-kohn puh sah-swahr ee-see⟩ — Can we sit here?

## 2. Ordering Drinks
In France, if you just order "un café," you will get a small, strong espresso. If you want a large American-style coffee, you have to specify.

| English Request | French Phrase | Pronunciation Guide |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| A (small) coffee | **Un café** | ⟨uhn kah-fay⟩ |
| A coffee with milk | **Un café crème** | ⟨uhn kah-fay krem⟩ |
| An American coffee | **Un café allongé** | ⟨uhn kah-fay ah-lohn-zhay⟩ |
| A glass of tap water (Free) | **Une carafe d'eau** | ⟨oon kah-rahf doh⟩ |
| A pint of beer | **Une pinte** | ⟨oon pahnt⟩ |
| A glass of red/white wine | **Un verre de vin rouge/blanc** | ⟨uhn ver duh van roozh/blahn⟩ |

*To order politely, simply add "s'il vous plaît" to the end of any of these phrases.*

## 3. During the Meal
Waiters in Paris typically leave you alone once the food arrives. If you need something, you must catch their eye and politely say "S'il vous plaît." Never snap your fingers or yell "Garçon!".

* **Excusez-moi** ⟨ex-koo-zay-mwah⟩ — Excuse me.
* **C'est délicieux!** ⟨say day-lee-syuh⟩ — It's delicious!
* **Où sont les toilettes ?** ⟨oo sohn lay twah-let⟩ — Where are the restrooms?

## 4. Paying the Bill
In France, the waiter will almost never bring the check to your table until you explicitly ask for it. It is considered rude to rush you.

* **Le compte, s'il vous plaît.** ⟨luh kohmt, seel voo play⟩ — The bill, please (more common in cafes).
* **L'addition, s'il vous plaît.** ⟨lah-dee-syon, seel voo play⟩ — The check, please (standard everywhere).
* **Je peux payer par carte ?** ⟨zhuh puh pay-yay par kahrt⟩ — Can I pay by card?

**Note on Tipping:** Service (*service compris*) is legally included in every bill in France. Leaving a tip is entirely optional and is only done for exceptional service (usually leaving a 1 or 2 euro coin on the table).
