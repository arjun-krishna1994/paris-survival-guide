# 7. Opinions & Small Talk

Parisians love a good debate. The quickest way to engage in a deeper conversation is to confidently express what you think.

## 1. Small Talk: The Weather
The universe's most reliable icebreaker. The verb **faire** (to do/make) is used to describe the weather in French.

| Phrase | French | Pronunciation Guide |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| It's hot. | **Il fait chaud** | ⟨eel fay shoh⟩ |
| I am hot. | **J'ai chaud** | ⟨zhay shoh⟩ |
| It's freezing! | **Il fait un froid de canard!** | ⟨eel fay uhn frwah duh kah-nahr⟩ |
| It's raining strings (pouring). | **Il pleut des cordes!** | ⟨eel pluh day kohrd⟩ |

*Wait, why "I have hot" instead of "I am hot"?* In French, you possess physical sensations. Saying "Je suis chaud" (I am hot) means something *very* different (slang for "I'm horny"). Stick to **J'ai chaud**.

## 2. Expressing Opinions
Instead of just stating facts, wrap your thoughts in these phrases to sound more native and polite.

* **À mon avis...** ⟨ah mohn ah-vee⟩ — In my opinion...
* **Je pense que...** ⟨zhuh pahns kuh⟩ — I think that...
* **Selon moi...** ⟨suh-lohn mwah⟩ — According to me...
* **Je suis d'accord.** ⟨zhuh swee dah-kohr⟩ — I agree.
* **Je ne suis pas d'accord.** ⟨zhuh nuh swee pah dah-kohr⟩ — I disagree.
* **C'est pas mal.** ⟨say pah mahl⟩ — That's not bad. (*Note: French people love understating things. "C'est pas mal" often means "It's fantastic!"*)

## 3. Essential Conversational Bridges
These filler phrases keep the conversation flowing naturally while you think of your next word.

* **Ah bon?** ⟨ah bohn⟩ — Oh really? / Is that so? (Very common reaction).
* **C'est-à-dire?** ⟨say-tah-deer⟩ — What do you mean by that? / That is to say?
* **Du coup...** ⟨doo koo⟩ — So... / As a result... (The ultimate colloquial filler word).
* **Bref...** ⟨bref⟩ — Anyway... / In short...
