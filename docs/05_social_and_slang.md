# 5. Social Bonding & Parisian Slang

If you want to move beyond basic tourist interactions and actually make a local friend, you'll need the vocabulary for socializing, plus a little bit of street cred.

## 1. Making Friends
When speaking to peers in a social setting (like a bar or party), it is acceptable to use the informal *Tu*.

* **Salut! / Coucou!** ⟨sah-loo / koo-koo⟩ — Hi! / Hey! (Casual).
* **Enchanté(e)!** ⟨ahn-shahn-tay⟩ — Nice to meet you!
* **D'où viens-tu ?** ⟨doo vyan-too⟩ — Where are you from?
* **Je viens des États-Unis / d'Angleterre.** ⟨zhuh vyan dayz ay-tahz-oo-nee / dahn-gluh-tair⟩ — I am from the US / England.
* **Ça te dit qu'on aille prendre un verre ?** ⟨sah tuh dee kohn ahy prahndr uhn ver⟩ — Do you feel like grabbing a drink?

## 2. Navigating Affection: The "Bien" Trap
This is a linguistic minefield for English speakers. In English, adding words usually makes the feeling stronger. In French, adding "bien" to "I like you" actually downgrades it to the friend zone.

| French Phrase | Literal Translation | Actual Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Je t'aime** | I love you | Serious romantic love. |
| **Je t'aime bien** | I like you well | "I like you as a friend." (Platonic). |
| **Tu me plais** | You please me | "I like you / I have a crush on you." (Romantic). |

## 3. Social Media
* **Tu as WhatsApp ?** ⟨too ah waht-sahp⟩ — Do you have WhatsApp?
* **C'est quoi ton insta ?** ⟨say kwah tohn an-stah⟩ — What's your Instagram? (Very common).

## 4. Parisian Youth Slang & Verlan
*Verlan* is a famous French slang technique where the syllables of words are swapped (l'envers -> vers-l'en -> verlan). Younger Parisians use it constantly.

* **C'est ouf!** ⟨say oof⟩ — That's crazy/awesome! (Verlan of *fou* = crazy).
* **Une meuf / Un mec** ⟨oon muhf / uhn mek⟩ — A woman/girl / a guy/dude. (Meuf is Verlan of *femme*).
* **C'est lourd** ⟨say loor⟩ — That's awesome / dope (Literally: That's heavy).
* **Je te kiffe** ⟨zhuh tuh keef⟩ — I really like you / I dig you (Arabic origin slang).
