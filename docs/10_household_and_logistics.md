# 10. Renting & Household Logistics

If you decide to stay in Paris longer than a few weeks, renting an apartment (*un appartement*) involves specific terminology and legal concepts.

## 1. The Apartment Hunt (La Recherche)
French real estate uses abbreviations. A studio is a studio. A "T1" or "F1" is a one-room apartment (main room + separate kitchen/bathroom). A "T2" is a two-room (living room + 1 bedroom).

| French Concept | English Meaning | Pronunciation Guide |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Le loyer** | The rent | ⟨luh lwah-yay⟩ |
| **Charges comprises (CC)** | Utilities included | ⟨sharzhe kohm-preez⟩ |
| **Hors charges (HC)** | Utilities not included | ⟨ohr sharzhe⟩ |
| **Le dépôt de garantie** | Security deposit | ⟨luh day-poh duh gah-rahn-tee⟩ |
| **La caution** | Guarantor (or deposit) | ⟨lah koh-syon⟩ |

* *Note:* In France, landlords almost always require a *garant* (guarantor) who earns 3x the rent and lives in France, or you must use a service like Garantme.

## 2. Moving In & Out
The most critical part of renting in France is the inventory report.

* **L'état des lieux** ⟨lay-tah day lyuh⟩ — The inventory of fixtures. You do one at entry (*d'entrée*) and exit (*de sortie*). Take photos of absolutely everything!
* **Le préavis** ⟨luh pray-ah-vee⟩ — The notice period before moving out (usually 1 month for furnished, 3 months for unfurnished).

## 3. Dealing with Issues
If something breaks in your apartment, you need to contact your landlord (*le propriétaire*) or the agency (*l'agence*).

* **J'ai un problème avec...** ⟨zhay uhn proh-blem ah-vek⟩ — I have a problem with...
* **La fuite d'eau** ⟨lah fweet doh⟩ — Water leak.
* **Le chauffage ne marche pas** ⟨luh shoh-fahzh nuh marsh pah⟩ — The heating is not working.
* **L'ascenseur est en panne** ⟨lah-sahn-soor ay ahn pahn⟩ — The elevator is broken.
* **J'ai perdu mes clés** ⟨zhay pair-doo may klay⟩ — I lost my keys.

## 4. Administrative Must-Haves
* **La quittance de loyer** ⟨lah kee-tahns duh lwah-yay⟩ — Rent receipt. You must ask for this every month. It serves as your official proof of address (*Justificatif de domicile*) for everything from opening a bank account to getting a cell phone contract.
* **Une assurance habitation** ⟨oon ah-soo-rahns ah-bee-tah-syon⟩ — Home insurance. It is legally required before you can be handed the keys.
